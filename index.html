The Challenge

The US website wasn't just badly translated. It was a full copy of the Korean version: the page structures, the product images, even the model photography followed Korean e-commerce conventions. American consumers couldn't connect with it. My senior didn't want me spending time on this because results wouldn't show quickly, but I knew the site was undermining everything else we were doing.

What I Did

Benchmarked US competitors and identified UX patterns American shoppers expected: shorter copy, toggle layouts, reviews above the fold. Rewrote product pages to feel natural, not translated. Directed photoshoots with diverse models. Built an article content section from scratch. Started with the highest-traffic pages, proved results, then scaled.

Olive Young SEA Performance Marketing
Ran A/B tests on creatives and messaging across 3 markets, defined what outperformed in each, and restructured campaigns per market to cut waste and scale winners.
+53%

The Challenge

Olive Young was expanding into Southeast Asia and needed performance marketing that could work across Singapore, Philippines, and Malaysia. Each market had different consumer behaviors, price sensitivities, and content preferences.

What I Did

Managed ad accounts across Meta and TikTok in all three markets. Ran A/B creative tests on visual tone (dark vs. pastel backgrounds) and messaging angles (discount-focused vs. product-focused). Found that dark-tone, discount-forward creatives outperformed across every market, while Philippines responded strongest to explicit price points over percentage discounts.

In Malaysia, identified that catalog and promotional campaigns were cannibalizing each other's budget inside a single campaign. Split them into separate campaigns, which freed up spend for priority promotions and improved catalog ROAS from 122% on relaunch to 1,786% by end of month.

In Philippines, developed a luxury targeting campaign focused on higher-income demographics that improved overall purchase efficiency.
