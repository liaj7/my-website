The Challenge

The US site wasn't just badly translated. It followed Korean e-commerce conventions: page structures, product images, model photography. American consumers couldn't trust it, and the data showed it. High bounce rates, low scroll depth, poor page value.

What I Did
  • Benchmarked US competitors (Glossier, The Ordinary, Drunk Elephant)
  • Rewrote product pages for American shoppers: shorter copy, toggle layouts, reviews above fold
  • Directed photoshoots with diverse models to reflect US audience
  • Built article content section from scratch to increase time-on-site
  • Started with highest-traffic pages, proved results, then scaled across the full site
