US Website Localization
The US site was a carbon copy of the Korean version. No amount of ad spend would fix that trust gap. I pushed for a full overhaul nobody asked for.
The Challenge

The US site wasn't just badly translated. It followed Korean e-commerce conventions: page structures, product images, model photography. American consumers couldn't trust it, and the data showed it. High bounce rates, low scroll depth, poor page value.

What I Did
  • Benchmarked US competitors (Glossier, The Ordinary, Drunk Elephant)
  • Rewrote product pages for American shoppers: shorter copy, toggle layouts, reviews above fold
  • Directed photoshoots with diverse models to reflect US audience
  • Built article content section from scratch to increase time-on-site
  • Started with highest-traffic pages, proved results, then scaled across the full site

Email Marketing System

APR had no email marketing for the US market. Generic templates, no segmentation, every promotion manually edited from scratch.

Olive Young SEA Performance Marketing
Three markets, three different audiences, one question: what actually works here? A/B tested creative and messaging per market. CTR up 30%, CVR up 44% — while spend scaled 59%.
The Challenge

Olive Young expanding into Southeast Asia needed performance marketing across Singapore, Philippines, and Malaysia. Each market had different consumer behaviors, price sensitivities, and content preferences. A one-size-fits-all campaign wouldn't work.

What I Did
  • Managed ad accounts across Meta and TikTok in Singapore, Philippines, and Malaysia
  • A/B tested visual tone (dark vs. pastel) and messaging (discount vs. product-focused)
  • Philippines: built a luxury targeting campaign for higher-income demographics
  • Malaysia: restructured catalog campaigns, significantly improving ROAS after creative and targeting overhaul
